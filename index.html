The New Detroit

1990-Present

After years of decay, increasing crime, "white flight", bankruptcy, and crooked politics, Detroit is now undergoing a huge turnaround. Tech companies and real estate investors are attracted to the city for its low priced downtown real estate and educated regional workforce.

  • 2014 population: 680,250
  • $26,325 median income
  • 39% Below Poverty
  • 10.6% White, 82.7% Black, 6.8% Hispanic

The Civil Rights Era

1960-1970

Detroit played a major role in the Civil Rights Movement of the 1960s. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. delivered his I Have a Dream speech for the first time at Cobo Hall, during the Walk to Freedom march.The march drew crowds of an estimated 125,000 and was the largest civil rights demonstration in U.S. history at the time.

  • 1960 population: 1,670,144
  • 5th largest city in the United States (1960)
  • Detroit's land size is now 139.6 square miles
  • 70.8% White, 28.9% Black

The twenties

1920-1930

The is often referred to as Detroit's Golden Era. Employment was high and the Detroit Automakers were making Detroit a world class city. The newly created wealth sparked opulent and beautiful architecture in the form of new shopping developments and historical cultural centers.

  • 1920 population 993,678 to 1.5 million in 1930
  • 95.8% White, 4.1% Black

Industrial Detroit

1890-1901

Detroit was earning a reputation as an important and diverse industrial and manufacturing center long before the arrival of the automobile industry. Advantages of streetcars propelled by electric power were beginning to be recognized over horse power. This time period is known for street car companies and stove manufacturers.

  • 1890 population: 205,876
  • 15th largest city in United States (1890)
  • Annexation increased Detroit's land size to 28.35 square miles
  • 98.3% White, 1.7% Black
