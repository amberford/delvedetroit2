The New Detroit

1990-Present

After years of decay, increasing crime, "white flight", bankruptcy, and crooked politics, Detroit is now undergoing a huge turnaround. Tech companies and real estate investors are attracted to the city for its low priced downtown real estate and educated regional workforce.

  • 2014 population: 680,250
  • $26,325 median income
  • 39% Below Poverty
  • 10.6% White, 82.7% Black, 6.8% Hispanic

The Civil Rights Era

1960-1970

Detroit played a major role in the Civil Rights Movement of the 1960s. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. delivered his I Have a Dream speech for the first time at Cobo Hall, during the Walk to Freedom march. King was accompanied by Reverend C.L. Franklin, father of singer and songwriter Aretha Franklin. The march drew crowds of an estimated 125,000 and was the largest civil rights demonstration in U.S. history at the time.

  • 1960 population: 1,670,144
  • 5th largest city in the United States (1960)
  • Detroit's land size is now 139.6 square miles
  • 70.8% White, 28.9% Black

Industrial Detroit

1890-1901

Detroit was earning a reputation as an important and diverse industrial and manufacturing center long before the arrival of the automobile industry. Advantages of streetcars propelled by electric power were beginning to be recognized over horse power. This time period is known for street car companies and stove manufacturers. By 1901, railroads linked Detroit to every part of the United States.

  • 1890 population: 205,876
  • 15th largest city in United States (1890)
  • Annexation increased Detroit's land size to 28.35 square miles
  • 98.3% White, 1.7% Black
