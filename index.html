The New Detroit

1990-Present

After years of decay, increasing crime, white flight, bankruptcy, and crooked politics, Detroit is increasingly becoming a city that is prime for a huge turnaround. Tech companies and real estate investors are attracted to the city for its low priced downtown real estate and educated regional workforce.

  • 2014 population 680,250
  • $26,325 median income
  • 39% Below Poverty
  • 10.6% White, 82.7% Black, 6.8% Hispanic

Industrial Detroit

1890-1900

Detroit was earning a reputation as an important and diverse industrial and manufacturing center long before the arrival of the automobile industry. Advantages of streetcars propelled by electric power were beginning to be recognized over horse power. This time period is known for street car companies and Hazen S. Pingree who served as mayor from 1890-1897.

  • 1890 population 205,876
  • 15th largest city in United States(1890).
  • Annexation increased Detroit's land size to 28.35 sq. miles
  • 98.3% White, 1.7% Black
