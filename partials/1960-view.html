THE MOTOWN SOUND

In 1959, boxer, assembly line worker and songwriter Berry Gordy Jr. used an $800 family loan to start a record company in Detroit. Motown achieved spectacular success for a small record company: 79 records in the Top Ten of the Billboard Hot 100 record chart between 1960 and 1969. By 1966, Motown grossed $20 million dollars.

Motown played an important role in the racial integration of pop music as a Black-owned record label with significantly crossover success. In the 1960s, Motown and its subsidiary labels were the most successful proponents of what came to be known as the Motown Sound, a style of soul music with a distinct pop influence.

Popular Motown artists include Marvin Gaye, The Temptations, The Supremes, Stevie Wonder, The Four Tops and The Miracles.

Viola Liuzzo

Viola Liuzzo, a Detroit housewife and civil rights activist, was shot and killed by four Ku Klux Klan members in Alabama. She was driving civil rights workers home after Dr. Martin Luther King’s Selma Freedom March.
