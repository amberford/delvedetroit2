Timeline Overview

  • 1890 - Hazen S. Pingree, a shoe manufacturer, is elected mayor of Detroit
  • 1894 - Mayor Pingree establishes vegetable gardens for the poor, which come to be known as Pingree's Potato Patches
  • 1896 - Charles Brady King drives Detroit’s first gasoline powered car down Woodward Avenue
  • 1897 - Sebastian S. Kresge opens his 5 and 10 cent store with J.G. McCrory, it develops into present day Kmart
  • 1897 - Mayor Pingree is elected governor of Michigan, forcing him to resign as Detroit's mayor
  • 1898 - Henry Ford organizes the Detroit Automobile Co. with backing from local investors. It fails three years later after only producing 2 cars
  • 1899 - Ransom E. Olds opens Detroit's first automobile manufacturing plant on East Jefferson
  • 1900 - Nearly 12% of Detroit’s population doesn’t speak English, the highest percentage in the nation
  • 1900 - The Detroit Tigers become members of the new American Baseball League
  • 1900 - Detroit is the world’s largest manufacturer of heating and cooking stoves

Pingree Years

Hazen S. Pingree served as Detroit mayor from 1890 to 1897, leaving office only after being elected governor of Michigan. Old Ping, as he was known, was named one of the 10 best mayors in U.S. history in a poll of scholars for Melvin Holli’s 1999 book, “The American Mayor.”

In it, Holli writes that Pingree’s “role as an advanced social reformer was unmatched by any big-city mayor in the last half of the 19th century.” His tough-love form of social reform, historians note, was the forerunner for the reforms of the Progressive Era. Besides his social reforms, the Republican mayor is best known for turning vacant land into vegetable patches to feed the city’s needy during the economic downturn of the 1890s.
