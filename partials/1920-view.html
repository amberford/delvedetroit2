Detroit's Golden Age

This was a time of great economic growth in America; and it was especially good to Detroit. The city underwent its most rapid growth in the 1920s--not coincidentally, the same period when automobile production skyrocketed. Detroit continued to be a magnet for newcomers until the early 1950s, when the city's population peaked at nearly two million.

The Purple Gang

The Purple Gang, Detroit’s most notorious organized crime gang in the 1920s and 1930s.

David Whitney Building

The building is named in honor of David Whitney Jr., who is responsible for much of Detroit's early commercial and industrial development. He was one of Detroit’s wealthiest men and largest landowners.

Prohibition

The Purple Gang began by hijacking alcohol smuggled by others across the Canadian border during prohibition.

Detroit Streetcars

Detroit had the largest streetcar system in the nation. The streetcar system was consolidated under the Detroit United Railway company.

Eastern Market

The Eastern Market presently covers 43 acres and is the largest historic public market district in the United States.
