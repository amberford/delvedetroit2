Detroit's Golden Age

This was a time of great economic growth in America and this time period was especially good to Detroit's economy. Fueled by the giant success in the Auto Industry Detroit was known the world over as an economic power. This spurred the building of new office buildings, cultural buildings, entertainment and cultural centers, as well as major shopping centers.

David Whitney Building

The building is named in honor of David Whitney Jr.responsible for much of Detroits early commercial and industrial development. He was one of Detroit’s wealthiest men and largest landowners.

The Purple Gang

The Purple Gang, made up of immigrants, was Detroit’s most notorious organized crime gang in the 1920s and 1930s. Criminal justice entities were rendered useless as they tried to control this gang's influence on Detroit.

Prohibition

The Purple Gang began by hijacking alcohol smuggled by others across the Canadian border during prohibition.

Eastern Market

Eastern Market grew greatly and at the market presently covers 43 acres and is the largest historic public market district in the United States.

Detroit Streetcars

Detroit had the largest streetcar system in the nation. The streetcar system was consolidated under the Detroit United Railway company.

Streets

In the 1920s Michigan Central Station was bustling with over 400 trains pulling in and out daily. This brought people from surrounding areas to work and shop in downtown Detroit.
